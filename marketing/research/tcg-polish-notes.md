# Dragon Cards TCG Polish Notes

## Research Signals

- MTG Arena presents a clear loop for new players: tutorial, profile setup, starter decks, legal formats, ranked play, events, daily rewards, and collection growth. Its Brawl format also shows how a commander-style identity mode can sit beside standard constructed play.
- Yu-Gi-Oh! Master Duel leans on Solo Mode as both tutorial and strategy training, with loaner decks and focused lessons for complex systems.
- Hearthstone markets around fast onboarding, premade decks, casual and ranked audiences, and broad mode variety. Its live updates add events, deck recipes, reward tracks, and temporary modes.
- Marvel Snap succeeds by keeping the session promise extremely clear: small decks, quick matches, strong visual feedback, and easy-to-learn rules.
- Final Fantasy TCG combat emphasizes active/dull states, attack declaration, blocker declaration, and priority windows. Its appeal comes from tactical response moments and readable board state.

## Dragon Cards Translation

- Keep the current 50-card Dragon Duel as the baseline, but make Mode Select the main front door so players immediately understand that there are casual, starter, avatar, sealed, tutorial, and multiplayer paths.
- Treat the deck builder as a collection browser, not just a list. It should surface legality, owned copies, missing cards, suggested replacements, and clear readable card details.
- Make combat feel interactive through explicit attack/block prompts, clear legal highlights, response windows, and audio/visual beats for attack, block, damage, and effects.
- Preserve progression integrity, but make sandbox instantly available for experimentation. Successful TCGs let players either grind collection or play with low-friction loaner/temporary decks.
- Continue building solo/tutorial content. The fastest way to widen the audience is guided play that rewards completion and teaches one concept at a time.

## Highest Impact Next Improvements

- Add deck recipes and "build from owned cards" suggestions for each element/playstyle.
- Add daily/weekly quests with small coin/pack rewards.
- Add a clearer post-match timeline: what was drawn, played, attacked, blocked, destroyed, looted, and rewarded.
- Add field polish: card hover lift, attack arrows, blocker lines, damage bursts, and rarity/card-play animations.
- Add player identity cosmetics later: sleeves, avatar frames, boards, and title badges.
- Add solo challenge ladders per element and expansion, with loaner decks and one-time rewards.
- Add mode-specific matchmaking/lobby labels so multiplayer players know whether they are playing Dragon Duel, Starter Clash, Dragon Avatar, Sealed, or Sandbox.
